Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. What are the best practices for storing fresh produce?

Answer: Fresh produce like fruits and vegetables should be stored in a cool, dry place or in the refrigerator. For leafy greens, it's best to store them in airtight containers with a paper towel to absorb moisture. Keep fruits like apples and bananas separate to avoid faster ripening.

2. How can I ensure food safety when cooking at home?

Answer: Always wash your hands before handling food, cook meat to the appropriate temperature, avoid cross-contamination by using separate cutting boards for meat and vegetables, and refrigerate leftovers within two hours of cooking.

3. What are some tips for meal prepping?

Answer: Plan your meals for the week ahead, cook large batches of staple items like grains and proteins, use airtight containers for storage, and label everything with the preparation date to ensure freshness.

4. What is the difference between organic and non-organic foods?

Answer: Organic foods are grown without synthetic pesticides, fertilizers, and GMOs, while non-organic foods may use these practices. Organic foods may be fresher but can also be more expensive due to the farming process.

5. How do I know if a food has gone bad?

Answer: Common signs include changes in smell, texture, and color. Mold, a sour or unpleasant odor, and off-putting flavors are strong indicators that food is no longer safe to consume.
